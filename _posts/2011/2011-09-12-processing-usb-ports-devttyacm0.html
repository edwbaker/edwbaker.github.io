--- layout: post title: "Processing and USB ports /dev/ttyACM0, /dev/ttyACM1, ...." date: 2011-09-12 21:39:00 source: pblog tags: ["Serial", "USB", "Arduino", "Processing"] redirect_from: - "/2011/09/processing-usb-ports-devttyacm0.html" --- Processing doesn't like reading Linux USB devices like /dev/ttyACM0 or /dev/ttyACM1.

The solution is easy, just symlink the devices  like this...

ln -s /dev/ttyACM[x] /dev/ttyS8[x]

You can put any number after the ttyS, although it is possible that ttyS0 and other low numbers are defined, numbers in the eighties are (almost) guaranteed to work.
