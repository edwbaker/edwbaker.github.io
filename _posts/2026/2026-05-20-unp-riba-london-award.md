The [Urban Nature Project](/urban-nature-project) at the Natural History Museum has been recognised with a **2026 UK RIBA London Award**.

The [RIBAJ award citation](https://www.ribaj.com/riba-awards/2026/london-south-west/feilden-fowles-urban-nature-project-natural-history-museum-mixed-kensington) describes how the redesign of the museum's gardens creates a "living exhibition" and a more accessible, biodiverse and educational landscape in South Kensington.

The project by [Feilden Fowles](https://www.feildenfowles.co.uk/projects/urban-nature-project-natural-history-museum/) for the Natural History Museum was also recognised in the 2026 UK RIBA London **Project Architect of the Year** category.

The judges highlighted the project's collaborative approach, ecological uplift and careful use of natural materials, including sustainable design choices made across both the gardens and new buildings.

It's great to see this work recognised as an example of how heritage sites in dense cities can support biodiversity, public engagement and long-term environmental resilience.
