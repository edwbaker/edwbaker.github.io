We have deployed a [LepiSense](https://nerc-ceh.github.io/lepisense-website/) device as part of the [Urban Research Station](/urban-research-station).

{% include figure.html img="/imgs/lepisense-urban-research-station.JPEG" description="LepiSense device deployed at the Urban Research Station" caption="LepiSense device deployed at the Urban Research Station." %}

LepiSense is a scalable automated monitoring system for nocturnal insects developed by the [UK Centre for Ecology & Hydrology](https://www.ceh.ac.uk/) (UKCEH). The device uses a light to attract moths and other nocturnal insects, and a camera to automatically capture images of them. These images can then be analysed to identify species, providing a way to monitor nocturnal insect populations over time without the need for manual trapping and identification.
